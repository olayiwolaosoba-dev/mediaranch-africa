The Brief

From pitch
to premiere
in 22 weeks.

01 · Challenge

Six stories. Four continents. One cinematic throughline.

UNHCR and NLNG needed a flagship film that would tour festivals, broadcast in three regions and land as a standalone creative object — without reading like a brochure. The throughline was the risk: six subjects, deeply different lives, no obvious narrative spine.

02 · Approach

We wrote the spine before we packed the flights.

Three months of desk research, character interviews, and treatment iterations preceded a single frame. Once the spine — the idea of inherited rhythm — was locked, the shoot schedule and visual grammar followed cleanly.

03 · Production

A ten-person crew, four countries, 11 weeks on the road.

Shot on Sony FX6 with Zeiss Supreme Primes. Drone sequences on DJI Inspire 3. Interviews backlit with a single 600D RGB — restraint as style. Sound captured with Sennheiser 8060 booms and DPA lavs.

04 · Post

Six weeks edit · three weeks grade · two weeks score.

Cut in Resolve 18. Custom LUT pack for a unified savannah-amber palette across locations. Original orchestral score recorded with a twelve-piece string section in Lagos, mixed in Dolby 5.1.

05 · Outcome

Festival nods. Broadcast buys. And 4.2M views in four weeks.

Selected for Sundance, AFRIFF and BFI London. Acquired for regional broadcast across Africa and Europe. Now used as the anchor film of UNHCR's 2025 pioneers program.
